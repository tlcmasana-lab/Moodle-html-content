Module 01

Atomic Ions

Core Concept

An atomic (monatomic) ion is a single atom with a net charge. Charge arises because protons ≠ electrons. Loss of electrons → Cation (+)  |  Gain of electrons → Anion (−)

⊕ Cation — Positive Ion
Sodium loses 1 electron → net charge becomes +1. Protons still = 11, but electrons = 10.
⊖ Anion — Negative Ion
Chlorine gains 1 electron → net charge becomes −1. Protons still = 17, but electrons = 18.

Module 03

Naming Ionic Compounds

1
Name the cation first
Write the metal name exactly as on the periodic table. No suffix changes.
Na → sodium | Mg → magnesium | Al → aluminum
2
Anion: add -ide suffix
For monatomic anions, drop the element ending and add -ide. Polyatomic anions keep their own names.
Cl⁻ → chloride | SO₄²⁻ → sulfate
3
Stock system for variable metals
Transition metals with multiple oxidation states get a Roman numeral in parentheses indicating the charge.
Fe²⁺ → iron(II) | Fe³⁺ → iron(III)
4
No subscripts in the name
The name never indicates subscripts. Subscripts come from charge balance — not the name itself.
MgO → magnesium oxide (not "1:1" anywhere)
5
Charge balance for formulas
Criss-cross the charges as subscripts, then simplify so the total positive charge = total negative charge.
Al³⁺ + O²⁻ → Al₂O₃
